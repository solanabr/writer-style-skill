# Craft Profile schema (secondary / "author" personas)

A craft profile captures an author's **transferable writing craft and intellectual quality** — how they
think, teach, reason, and structure — so an agent can apply it **in the user's voice, never as
impersonation.** This is the "quality, not quirks" half of the system.

## The governing principle: quality, not quirks
**Strip the idiolect; keep the craft.** Anything that would mark text as *this specific author's* is
excluded; anything that is *generalizable good-writing craft* is kept.

| KEEP (transferable craft) | EXCLUDE (identifiable idiolect) |
|---|---|
| explanatory method, difficulty staging, abstraction/analogy logic, argument architecture, intellectual-honesty moves, quantification habits, structure-to-intent, reader-modeling, the author's signature *strengths* | punctuation tics (em-dash habits, spaced hyphens), signature phrases & connectives, ritual openers/closers, header/section naming conventions, personas, coined vocabulary, political/tonal stance, catchphrases |

Why: the end goal is *original* work in the user's voice. Borrowed fingerprints make output a pastiche
(derivative, and instantly recognizable as someone else). Borrowed *craft* makes it better while staying
the user's own. Stripped of fingerprints, profiles naturally **converge on universal craft and differ by
each author's signature strength**, which is exactly what you want feeding a blend.

Quotes in the profile are **evidence of a technique**, never templates to reproduce verbatim.

## Section schema (match the existing `personas/*.md`)
Keep it lean: depth where depth changes output, lean everywhere else.

1. **Header**: one line: "Writing Craft Profile — distilled from [Author]." State it's craft, not
   impersonation, applied in the user's voice; idiolect excluded (see §last).
2. **"What to lift from them specifically"**: name the author's **signature strengths** up front (2–4).
   The profile leads with what this author does *better than most*; that's its reason to exist.
3. **How they explain / teach**: their core explanatory method as a repeatable sequence; difficulty
   staging (simplify-then-recomplicate, worked-example-as-explanation, notation laddering, recap, honesty
   scaffolding); abstraction & analogy (motivate-before-define; analogy that carries the idea's
   *structure* and flags where it breaks; define-by-poles + tables).
4. **How they reason / argue**: intellectual-honesty moves (calibrated conviction, steelman, "compared
   to what?", chain-to-conclusions, name-the-tradeoff), and how they establish authority.
5. **How they structure**: argument architectures / genre awareness relevant to the use-case;
   openings/closings logic; reader-modeling.
6. **Quantification / evidence** (if relevant): how they prove claims (ratios + paired cost, exact
   figures + units, reproducibility).
7. **Generation guidance + a worked example IN NEUTRAL VOICE**: a checklist to apply the craft, then a
   short worked passage on a use-case topic that *demonstrates the craft* without any of the author's
   wording, punctuation tics, or persona. Annotate which craft moves it used. The neutral voice is the
   point: it shows the craft is separable from the author and ready to wear the user's voice.
8. **"What this profile deliberately EXCLUDES"**: explicitly list the author's idiolect that must NOT be
   reproduced. This discipline is what keeps output original; never omit it.

## Quality checks specific to craft profiles
- Could a reader who knows the author tell the worked example was "by" them? If yes, you kept idiolect.
  Cut it.
- Is every section a *mechanism* ("rules out naive solutions first") or a *number*, not an adjective?
- Does it lead with the signature strength, and is that strength genuinely distinctive vs the other
  personas (not "explains clearly," which everyone does)?
- After the adversarial cut: is it leaner than the draft *and* did you fold in the gap-examples the
  advocate found? (Both should be true.)
- Did you verify any frequency counts against the corpus? (The hawk/judges catch inflated tics — e.g., a
  pattern that's really a relic of one era or one genre.)

## Anti-patterns
- A bag of adjectives ("witty, rigorous, engaging"), describes nothing actionable.
- Idiolect smuggled in as "craft" (e.g., "uses lots of em-dashes" is a tic, not craft; the *craft* is
  whatever the em-dash is doing — apposition, reframe — stated mechanism-first and detached from the dash).
- A profile so comprehensive it's indistinguishable from the others. Over-cut the shared craft, sharpen
  the distinctive strength.
- No worked example, or a worked example in the author's voice (defeats the whole purpose).
