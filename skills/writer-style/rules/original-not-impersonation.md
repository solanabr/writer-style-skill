# Original, never impersonation

The output is the **primary author's own original writing** — that happens to be well-structured,
well-argued, well-paced because it borrowed *craft* from master writers. It is **not** a blend of other
people's voices.

## The inversion (get this right or everything fails)
- **PRIMARY voice (e.g. Kaue): REPRODUCE the idiolect.** The quirks, rhythm, signature moves, sign-offs — the
  asset. This is what makes it sound like *them*.
- **SECONDARY voices: STRIP the idiolect.** Borrow only *how they think / teach / structure*. Never reproduce
  their punctuation tics, slogans, coined terms, named-exemplar casts, or persona.

Strip the primary's idiolect → a "well-behaved impostor" (correct, generic, not them). Keep a secondary's
idiolect → a pastiche (the lesson reads like a George Hotz impression instead of Kaue using Hotz's
compression). Both are failures.

## In practice
- Apply Vitalik's **derivation method**, not his "kind of" / "Special thanks to …" / Alice-and-Bob.
- Apply Hayes's **stamina engineering**, not his trader identity, nicknames, or anti-fiat doom.
- Apply Balaji's **framing architecture**, not his coinages, rule-of-three anaphora, or prophetic register.
- Apply Hotz's **compression**, not his contempt posture, all-caps, or sarcasm-as-conclusion.
- Apply Helius's **structure & evidence**, not its ritual section labels or "if you've read this far, anon."

Each secondary profile ends with a **"What this deliberately EXCLUDES"** note and its card lists the idiolect
in `avoid:`. Those are the firewall — honor them. Craft is the menu; the surface voice stays Kaue's.

## Why it matters
Originality is the point — legally, ethically, and for quality. Borrowed craft makes the writing *better*;
borrowed idiolect makes it a forgery of someone who didn't write it. Keep the craft, drop the costume.
