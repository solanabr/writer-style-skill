Here's the stake for you as a long-term holder: whether your share of the network grows, holds, or quietly thins depends on one number you can actually look up — the inflation rate, the pace at which new SOL is minted each year.

It started near 8% and steps down about 15% a year toward a long-run 1.5%. Disinflation just means the faucet narrows on a schedule you can see coming.

Walk one round year. Say 500 million SOL exists and 8% — 40 million — is issued to stakers. Stake your share and you collect your slice; your ownership holds. Sit it out and your count is unchanged, but it's now a piece of 540 million.

To recap: 1) new SOL is minted on a known, shrinking schedule; 2) it flows to stakers; 3) staking keeps your share, sitting out slowly trims it.
